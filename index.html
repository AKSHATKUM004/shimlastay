Our Story

Where Every Wall
Whispers History

Nestled in the heart of Kasumpti, Shimla, Heritage Aira Holme stands as one of the oldest surviving colonial structures in the summer capital of British India. Originally erected circa 1870, this storied estate has witnessed over a century and a half of Himalayan seasons — its walls etched with the memories of a grander age.

The building's colonial architecture — characterized by pitched roofs, timber-framed verandahs, and arched windows — remains immaculately preserved. Every corridor breathes the ambiance of a bygone era, adorned with antique furnishings that tell tales of the British Raj and the golden years of Shimla's prominence.

Today, this distinguished heritage property opens its doors as an intimate homestay — inviting discerning travelers to live inside a piece of living history, within walking distance of the legendary Mall Road and The Ridge.

The Heritage Timeline

A Legacy Carved in Stone

Circa 1870
The Foundation Stone
Aira Holme is constructed in Kasumpti, Shimla — during the peak of British colonial rule when Shimla served as the summer capital of British India. Built with traditional colonial craftsmanship, the estate embodies the architectural grandeur of the Victorian era.
Late 1800s
Shimla's Golden Age
As Shimla flourishes as the nerve centre of British India's summer administration, Aira Holme stands among the city's most distinguished colonial residences — bearing witness to the social, political, and cultural life of the subcontinent's most powerful hill station.
Post Independence
A Family's Sacred Custodianship
The estate passes into the care of a heritage family who dedicate generations to its preservation. Original timber floors, arched windows, verandahs, and period furnishings are meticulously maintained — a labour of love across the decades.
2024 — Present
Heritage Homestay Opens Its Doors
Heritage Aira Holme welcomes the world — opening its historic chambers to travelers seeking an authentic, living-history experience. The estate becomes Shimla's most intimate colonial retreat, blending 150-year-old charm with the warmth of family hospitality.

The Aira Holme Experience

Live the Colonial Romance

01
🏔️
Himalayan Panoramas
Wake to breathtaking vistas of the surrounding Himalayan ranges — the same timeless view that greeted British officers a century and a half ago. The elevated position of Aira Holme offers unparalleled perspectives of Shimla's forested valleys and snow-capped peaks.
02
🌿
The Heritage Garden
Wander the estate's sprawling garden — a green sanctuary of seasonal blooms, century-old trees, and colonial landscaping. An ideal retreat for morning contemplation, afternoon reading, or simply absorbing the mountain air in a setting of extraordinary serenity.
03
🏛️
Colonial Architecture
The estate's original structure — pitched timber roofs, arched verandahs, ornate woodwork, and period-correct interiors — represents some of the finest surviving examples of Victorian colonial architecture in Shimla. A feast for the eyes and the imagination.
04
🍵
Himachali Hospitality
Experience the legendary warmth of a family-run heritage estate. Mornings begin with locally-crafted breakfast flavours, evenings are filled with personal stories of Shimla's history, and every interaction carries the genuine care of hosts who call this storied home their own.
05
🗺️
Near Mall Road & Ridge
Perfectly positioned in Kasumpti — a short distance from Shimla's iconic Mall Road, The Ridge, Christ Church, and Jakhu Temple. The estate offers the tranquility of a private retreat with the convenience of Shimla's most celebrated landmarks at your doorstep.
06
🕰️
A Living Museum
Every corner of Aira Holme tells a story. Antique furnishings, period paintings, vintage photography, and architectural details preserved across generations transform your stay into an immersive journey through 150 years of Shimla's extraordinary history.
